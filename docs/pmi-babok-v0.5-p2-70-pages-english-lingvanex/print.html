P2.1.1 What Is a Standard? (Tiêu chuẩn là gì?)

What it is

A standard provides a stable reference for commonly accepted practice. In Part 2, it frames business analysis as a disciplined way to move from uncertainty to informed product and business decisions. It gives the BA a structured way to reduce ambiguity and make product information usable by the wider team.

Why it is used

For P2-1-1, the value is to keep business goals, stakeholder intent, product scope, and delivery decisions aligned. Without it, teams may deliver quickly while still solving the wrong problem.

When to use it

Use this perspective when an organization wants consistency across projects, but still needs room to tailor artifacts, cadence, and governance to the context. Use P2-1-1 with extra care when the next decision may affect investment, scope, priority, design, testing, transition, or release confidence.
