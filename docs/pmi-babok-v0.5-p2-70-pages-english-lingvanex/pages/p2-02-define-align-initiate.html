P2.2.1 Identify Problem or Opportunity (Xác định vấn đề hoặc cơ hội)

What it is

This activity frames the reason for action. It turns a signal, complaint, idea, or opportunity into a business need and a situation statement. It gives the BA a structured way to reduce ambiguity and make product information usable by the wider team.

Why it is used

For P2-2-1, the value is to keep business goals, stakeholder intent, product scope, and delivery decisions aligned. Without it, teams may deliver quickly while still solving the wrong problem.

When to use it

Use it before selecting a solution, funding a project, or treating a stakeholder request as a confirmed requirement. Use P2-2-1 with extra care when the next decision may affect investment, scope, priority, design, testing, transition, or release confidence.

How to apply it

For P2-2-1, begin with the decision to support, inspect the available inputs, choose a technique that fits the uncertainty, create a reviewable output, and connect the result to the next BA or delivery activity.

Step-by-step application guide

  1. Frame the decision. State what the team needs to decide and what risk will remain if the decision is made with weak information.
  2. Inspect the inputs. Separate confirmed facts from assumptions, old documentation, stakeholder preferences, and missing evidence.
  3. Select a technique. Match the P2-2-1 method to the information need: discovery, comparison, modeling, prioritization, validation, or approval.
  4. Create a usable artifact. Keep the artifact clear enough for review and traceable enough to survive future changes.
  5. Close the loop. Confirm the result, record open questions, and update the appropriate product-information repository.

Practical example

When users say provider search is poor, the BA separates symptoms from causes: missing data, ranking logic, filters, specialty terminology, or unclear result presentation.

Common anti-patterns

  • Treating identify problem or opportunity as a form-filling exercise instead of a decision-support activity.
  • Failing to name assumptions, owners, and unresolved questions before the next handoff.
  • Creating a P2-2-1 artifact that is not reviewed, traced, or reused by product, delivery, testing, or release stakeholders.

  • Where will the result be traced, updated, or used next?

Agile / hybrid BA lens

In an Agile environment, P2-2-1 may appear as refinement notes, story-map updates, a decision record, a prototype annotation, or acceptance criteria. In a predictive or compliance-heavy environment, P2-2-1 may require a more formal document and approval path. The form should change with context; the reasoning should remain explicit.

Key takeaway: Identify Problem or Opportunity is valuable when it produces evidence that stakeholders actually use to decide, prioritize, design, test, transition, or release.

For Sprint 0, BA support ensures the charter reflects the business objective, product scope, stakeholders, assumptions, constraints, and initial product information needed by the team.

Common anti-patterns

  • Treating support charter development as a form-filling exercise instead of a decision-support activity.
  • Failing to name assumptions, owners, and unresolved questions before the next handoff.
  • Creating a P2-3-1 artifact that is not reviewed, traced, or reused by product, delivery, testing, or release stakeholders.

Quick checklist

  • What decision will this section support?
  • Which inputs are facts, assumptions, or stakeholder opinions?
  • Which technique best matches the type of uncertainty?
  • Who must review or approve the output?
  • Where will the result be traced, updated, or used next?

Agile / hybrid BA lens

In an Agile environment, P2-3-1 may appear as refinement notes, story-map updates, a decision record, a prototype annotation, or acceptance criteria. In a predictive or compliance-heavy environment, P2-3-1 may require a more formal document and approval path. The form should change with context; the reasoning should remain explicit.

Key takeaway: Support Charter Development is valuable when it produces evidence that stakeholders actually use to decide, prioritize, design, test, transition, or release.
