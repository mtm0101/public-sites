P2.5.9 Validate Requirements (Xác thực yêu cầu)

What it is

Validation confirms that requirements align with business goals and stakeholder value, not just that they are well written. It gives the BA a structured way to reduce ambiguity and make product information usable by the wider team.

Why it is used

For P2-5-9, the value is to keep business goals, stakeholder intent, product scope, and delivery decisions aligned. Without it, teams may deliver quickly while still solving the wrong problem.

When to use it

Use it when scope grows, feature requests compete, or stakeholders question why a requirement matters. Use P2-5-9 with extra care when the next decision may affect investment, scope, priority, design, testing, transition, or release confidence.

How to apply it

For P2-5-9, begin with the decision to support, inspect the available inputs, choose a technique that fits the uncertainty, create a reviewable output, and connect the result to the next BA or delivery activity.

Step-by-step application guide

  1. Frame the decision. State what the team needs to decide and what risk will remain if the decision is made with weak information.
  2. Inspect the inputs. Separate confirmed facts from assumptions, old documentation, stakeholder preferences, and missing evidence.
  3. Select a technique. Match the P2-5-9 method to the information need: discovery, comparison, modeling, prioritization, validation, or approval.
  4. Create a usable artifact. Keep the artifact clear enough for review and traceable enough to survive future changes.
  5. Close the loop. Confirm the result, record open questions, and update the appropriate product-information repository.

Practical example

A feature can be verified as clear but fail validation if it does not support the goal of reducing user time-to-find or increasing successful contact rates.

Common anti-patterns

  • Treating validate requirements as a form-filling exercise instead of a decision-support activity.
  • Failing to name assumptions, owners, and unresolved questions before the next handoff.
  • Creating a P2-5-9 artifact that is not reviewed, traced, or reused by product, delivery, testing, or release stakeholders.
