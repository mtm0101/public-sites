P2.6.1 Manage Stakeholder Engagement and Communication (Quản lý sự tham gia và giao tiếp của các bên liên quan)

What it is

This activity monitors stakeholder participation and adjusts communication as the initiative changes. It gives the BA a structured way to reduce ambiguity and make product information usable by the wider team.

Why it is used

For P2-6-1, the value is to keep business goals, stakeholder intent, product scope, and delivery decisions aligned. Without it, teams may deliver quickly while still solving the wrong problem.

When to use it

Use it throughout discovery, analysis, delivery, testing, transition, and release. Use P2-6-1 with extra care when the next decision may affect investment, scope, priority, design, testing, transition, or release confidence.

How to apply it

For P2-6-1, begin with the decision to support, inspect the available inputs, choose a technique that fits the uncertainty, create a reviewable output, and connect the result to the next BA or delivery activity.

Step-by-step application guide

  1. Frame the decision. State what the team needs to decide and what risk will remain if the decision is made with weak information.
  2. Inspect the inputs. Separate confirmed facts from assumptions, old documentation, stakeholder preferences, and missing evidence.
  3. Select a technique. Match the P2-6-1 method to the information need: discovery, comparison, modeling, prioritization, validation, or approval.
  4. Create a usable artifact. Keep the artifact clear enough for review and traceable enough to survive future changes.
  5. Close the loop. Confirm the result, record open questions, and update the appropriate product-information repository.

Practical example

If QA repeatedly receives stories too late, the BA changes the communication flow so QA reviews acceptance criteria before sprint commitment.

Common anti-patterns

  • Treating manage stakeholder engagement and communication as a form-filling exercise instead of a decision-support activity.
  • Failing to name assumptions, owners, and unresolved questions before the next handoff.
  • Creating a P2-6-1 artifact that is not reviewed, traced, or reused by product, delivery, testing, or release stakeholders.
