4.10.2 Purpose of Models
Business analysis models are helpful to ﬁnd gaps in information and to identify extraneous information. Models provide
context to better understand and more clearly convey information. Requirements are modeled and reﬁned to achieve further
clarity, correctness, and to elicit additional information to deﬁne the details necessary for the product to be built.
When the correct models are applied, analysis becomes simple relative to analyzing the information in pure
text form, because the models help visualize and summarize complex information. When the correct models are
applied, analysis becomes much easier.
