3.5. Plan the Business Analysis Work (Lập kế hoạch công việc BA)#

Chapter 3, pp. 61-68

Meaning and purpose

Plan the Business Analysis Work addresses the approach for conducting, coordinating, governing, and improving BA work. In the selected scope, it is treated as a practical BA activity that clarifies the business situation, prepares the team for requirements work, or supports investment and planning decisions.

Why it matters

It matters because weak analysis at this point can cause downstream requirements gaps, unclear stakeholder expectations, scope churn, or solution bias. This concept helps the practitioner convert ambiguous input into a decision-ready view.

When to use it

Use it when the team needs to understand or plan the approach for conducting, coordinating, governing, and improving BA work; when evidence is incomplete; or when stakeholders need a shared basis for the next decision.

How to apply it

Apply it by doing four things: define the decision it supports, gather enough evidence, involve the right stakeholders, and document the output in a way that can guide later BA work. Practically, tailor the approach to life cycle, complexity, stakeholders, and project risk.

Practical example: Example: in a software initiative, plan the business analysis work may shape which SMEs are interviewed, which acceptance criteria are needed, which risks are escalated, or which option is recommended before development begins.

Common mistakes / anti-patterns

  • creating a generic plan that does not guide behavior
  • moving to solution design before the business need and decision criteria are stable
  • producing an artifact that no stakeholder uses for a real decision
