2.4.5. Determine Required Capabilities Needed to Address the Situation (Xác định năng lực cần có)#

Chapter 2, pp. 25-27

Meaning and purpose

Determine Required Capabilities Needed to Address the Situation addresses organizational abilities across people, process, data, technology, policy, and structure. In the selected scope, it is treated as a practical BA activity that clarifies the business situation, prepares the team for requirements work, or supports investment and planning decisions.

Why it matters

It matters because weak analysis at this point can cause downstream requirements gaps, unclear stakeholder expectations, scope churn, or solution bias. This concept helps the practitioner convert ambiguous input into a decision-ready view.

When to use it

Use it when the team needs to understand or plan organizational abilities across people, process, data, technology, policy, and structure; when evidence is incomplete; or when stakeholders need a shared basis for the next decision.

How to apply it

Apply it by doing four things: define the decision it supports, gather enough evidence, involve the right stakeholders, and document the output in a way that can guide later BA work. Practically, compare required capability with current capability and prioritize significant gaps.

Practical example: Example: in a software initiative, determine required capabilities needed to address the situation may shape which SMEs are interviewed, which acceptance criteria are needed, which risks are escalated, or which option is recommended before development begins.
