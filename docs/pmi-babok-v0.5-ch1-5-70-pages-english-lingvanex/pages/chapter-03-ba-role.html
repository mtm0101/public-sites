Chapter 3: The Role of the Business Analyst (Vai trò của nhà phân tích kinh doanh)

Chapter 3 clarifies that the business analyst is not merely a job title. It is a role that influences product understanding, organizational decisions, stakeholder alignment, and professional learning.

Body content is English-only. Titles and terminology remain bilingual.

3.1 Overview (Tổng quan)

What it is

Describes the ba role as the person or function that helps an organization understand problems and create trustworthy product information. In practical BA work, this section turns vague observations into structured information that another person can review, test, or use in a product decision.

Why it matters

It prevents the ba from being reduced to a meeting scribe, secondary tester, or document writer. When it is performed well, the team reduces misunderstanding, avoids late rework, and increases the chance that the solution is connected to business value rather than only backlog completion.

When to use it

When defining project responsibilities or dividing work among po, pm, ux, qa, engineering, and ba roles. It becomes especially important when assumptions are unvalidated, when multiple stakeholder groups see the situation differently, or when the next decision will consume meaningful resources.

How to apply it

Start with the decision question: what does the team need to know in order to move forward safely? Then identify sources, choose an appropriate technique, document the result at the right level of detail, and confirm the result with people who have authority or direct experience.

Step-by-step application guide

  1. Frame the question. Write one sentence describing the decision to be supported; do not begin with the name of a preferred solution.
  2. Gather evidence. Combine documents, data, interviews, observations, or workshops to avoid relying on a single opinion.
  3. Structure the information. Use a table, model, story map, RACI, capability view, or decision log to expose relationships and dependencies.
  4. Confirm and adjust. Send the result back to the right stakeholders; separate confirmed facts, assumptions, and unresolved decisions.
  5. Connect to delivery. Convert the result into a backlog item, acceptance criteria, roadmap item, risk, action, or metric so it is not lost.

Practical example

In an Agile project, the Product Owner owns product priority, the Project Manager manages delivery coordination, UX shapes experience, and the BA clarifies needs, models product information, defines acceptance criteria, and validates requirements. If this split is unclear, the backlog can become a container for untested ideas.

Common mistakes

  • assigning all ambiguity to the BA without giving access to stakeholders
  • treating the BA as a translator who copies customer statements into tickets
  • excluding the BA from early discovery

Application checklist

  • Can the BA access users or SMEs?
  • Is the BA role in prioritization, analysis, and validation clear?
  • Are BA outputs used in testing, design, and release?
  • Who owns the final product decision?

BA lens in Agile or hybrid work

In Agile delivery, this content does not have to become a long document. It can appear in a story map, refinement note, prototype annotation, decision record, or acceptance criteria. In hybrid or predictive delivery, the same information may need a more formal form for approval, audit, or handoff. The quality criterion is not document size; it is whether the information is reliable, current, and useful for the next decision.

Key takeaway: Overview should produce evidence that helps the team decide, prioritize, design, test, or transition. If the artifact is not used, reduce its weight or change how it is presented.

Question 1 - Hard

A Chapter 3 initiative has stakeholders using different meanings for the same product goal. What should the BA do first to reduce rework risk?

  1. A. Ask only the sponsor because the sponsor has the highest authority
  2. B. Wait for QA to detect gaps after development is complete
  3. C. Create a shared view, confirm assumptions, and connect the output to the next decision
  4. D. Write all requirements immediately so engineering can begin
Show answer

Correct answer: C

The strongest action creates clarity and validates assumptions before they become scope.

Question 2 - Hard

In the context of The Role of the Business Analyst, which behavior shows that the guide is being applied too mechanically?

  1. A. Stakeholders are invited based on role and influence
  2. B. Artifacts are selected according to risk and life cycle
  3. C. Every project must use the same documentation set regardless of risk
  4. D. The technique changes when new evidence appears
Show answer

Correct answer: C

Tailoring matters; one heavy template for every situation usually creates waste.

Question 5 - Hard

During Chapter 3 review, the BA finds a recorded decision with no assumptions. What is the main risk?

Assumptions are the conditions that make a decision valid; if they change, the decision may need review.
