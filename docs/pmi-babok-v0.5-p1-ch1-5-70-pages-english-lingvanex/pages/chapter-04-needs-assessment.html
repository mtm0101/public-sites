Chapter 4: Needs Assessment (Đánh giá nhu cầu)

Chapter 4 is the chain of activities that moves an organization from a problem or opportunity to solution options, a roadmap, a business case, and charter support. It is the foundation before delivery commitments are made.

Body content is English-only. Titles and terminology remain bilingual.

4.1 Identify Problem or Opportunity (Xác định vấn đề hoặc cơ hội)

What it is

Turns an initial signal into a business need and a situation statement clear enough for further analysis. In practical BA work, this section turns vague observations into structured information that another person can review, test, or use in a product decision.

Why it matters

It prevents the team from jumping to a solution before understanding the real problem, affected parties, and business consequence. When it is performed well, the team reduces misunderstanding, avoids late rework, and increases the chance that the solution is connected to business value rather than only backlog completion.

When to use it

When a major request, product idea, customer complaint, performance drop, or market opportunity appears. It becomes especially important when assumptions are unvalidated, when multiple stakeholder groups see the situation differently, or when the next decision will consume meaningful resources.

How to apply it

Start with the decision question: what does the team need to know in order to move forward safely? Then identify sources, choose an appropriate technique, document the result at the right level of detail, and confirm the result with people who have authority or direct experience.

Step-by-step application guide

  1. Frame the question. Write one sentence describing the decision to be supported; do not begin with the name of a preferred solution.
  2. Gather evidence. Combine documents, data, interviews, observations, or workshops to avoid relying on a single opinion.
  3. Structure the information. Use a table, model, story map, RACI, capability view, or decision log to expose relationships and dependencies.
  4. Confirm and adjust. Send the result back to the right stakeholders; separate confirmed facts, assumptions, and unresolved decisions.
  5. Connect to delivery. Convert the result into a backlog item, acceptance criteria, roadmap item, risk, action, or metric so it is not lost.

Practical example

A customer says search results are poor. The BA distinguishes possible causes: weak data, ranking logic, filter design, specialty terminology, or unmanaged expectations. Only when the problem is framed correctly can the solution create value.

Common mistakes

  • writing the need as a preselected feature
  • not asking what happens if the issue is not solved
  • relying only on the opinion of the most powerful person

Application checklist

  • Is the problem or opportunity stated in business language?
  • Is there evidence or a signal behind it?
  • Are affected stakeholders and expected benefits identified?
  • Has the team avoided locking the solution too early?

BA lens in Agile or hybrid work

In Agile delivery, this content does not have to become a long document. It can appear in a story map, refinement note, prototype annotation, decision record, or acceptance criteria. In hybrid or predictive delivery, the same information may need a more formal form for approval, audit, or handoff. The quality criterion is not document size; it is whether the information is reliable, current, and useful for the next decision.

Key takeaway: Identify Problem or Opportunity should produce evidence that helps the team decide, prioritize, design, test, or transition. If the artifact is not used, reduce its weight or change how it is presented.

A Chapter 4 initiative has stakeholders using different meanings for the same product goal. What should the BA do first to reduce rework risk?

  1. A. Write all requirements immediately so engineering can begin

  3. C. Wait for QA to detect gaps after development is complete
  4. D. Create a shared view, confirm assumptions, and connect the output to the next decision

The strongest action creates clarity and validates assumptions before they become scope.
