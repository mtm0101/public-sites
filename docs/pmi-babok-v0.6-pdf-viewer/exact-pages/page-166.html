5.2.5 CONDUCT STAKEHOLDER ANALYSIS: COLLABORATION POINT
Project managers and business analysts encounter an overlap with regard to stakeholder analysis activities because
both roles have a vested interest in understanding stakeholder characteristics and managing stakeholders. On adaptive
life cycle projects, the overlap or interest and responsibility may exist between any roles performing this analysis. To ensure
that redundancy of effort is avoided, business analysts and portfolio, program, and project managers should complete this
work collaboratively. Each role provides a unique perspective, with portfolio, program, and project managers analyzing
stakeholders for impacts to the portfolio, program, and project, respectively, and business analysts analyzing stakeholders
for impacts to the solution and to ensure an effective business analysis process. For the sake of the stakeholders, the
product team needs to operate as a cohesive unit and avoid making uncoordinated and redundant requests of stakeholders.
